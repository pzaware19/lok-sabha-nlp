What Does India’s Parliament Actually Talk About?

Mapping the ideological landscape of Indian democracy from 150,000 parliamentary questions using unsupervised machine learning — no labels, no hand-coding, just text.

Piyush Zaware  ·  MPP, University of Chicago  ·  Applied Economics Researcher, Global Poverty Research Laboratory, Northwestern Kellogg

150,000 Questions

16th – 18th Lok Sabha

2014 – 2026

LDA · STM · Word2Vec · UMAP

Every parliamentary session, Indian MPs submit hundreds of written questions to government ministries. These are not speeches or debates — they are targeted, on-the-record demands for information. Taken together across three Lok Sabhas and 13 parties, they form a paper trail of what each party actually cares about: agriculture subsidies, minority rights, road infrastructure, defence procurement, labour rights.

This project asks a simple question: can you recover the ideological map of Indian politics purely from how MPs phrase their questions, without any human annotation? Using topic models, word embeddings, and clustering, the answer is yes — and the results are sharper than expected.

The Data

150K

Total parliamentary questions, 16th–18th Lok Sabha

13

Parties: BJP, INC, Left, BSP, AAP, TMC, SP, JDU, DMK, TDP, RJD, Shiv Sena + Regional

205

Documents in the corpus (one per party per parliamentary session)

The dataset is the opensansad/lok-sabha-qa corpus on HuggingFace — 150,629 questions covering the 16th (2014–2019), 17th (2019–2024), and 18th (2024–) Lok Sabha. The analysis focuses on starred (oral) questions, which are richer in political content than unstarred written submissions.

MP-to-party mapping was built by scraping Wikipedia’s constituency lists for all three Lok Sabhas, yielding a 38% match rate — sufficient to cover BJP, INC, and most major opposition parties robustly.

Methodology

The pipeline runs end-to-end in R with a single source("code/_master.R") call.

Method What it does Output
LDA Discovers 15 recurring topics across all questions Topic weights per party
STM Like LDA but with party and time as covariates BJP vs INC contrasts; temporal trends
Word2Vec + Kozlowski (2019) Encodes ideological dimensions as directions in embedding space Party positions on 3 axes
HAC + GMM Clusters parties by linguistic similarity Natural party groupings
UMAP / t-SNE Projects 3,258-dimensional TF-IDF space to 2D Visual map of the corpus
Note

On the Kozlowski method: ideological dimensions are defined by antonym pairs — e.g. the Hindutva dimension is the vector direction from {secular, minority, constitution} toward {hindu, mandir, cow, ram}. Party positions are then the TF-IDF-weighted average projection of their vocabulary onto this direction. See Kozlowski, Taddy & Evans (2019), American Sociological Review.

Key Findings

The Ideological Map

Word embeddings alone recover a recognisable political structure from parliamentary language, with no human annotation.

The structure is exactly what a political scientist would draw by hand. BJP and Shiv Sena sit at the Hindu-nationalist pole; Left parties and AAP at the secular end; INC occupies the centre-left, consistent with its broad coalition identity. On the economic axis, SP and BSP use strongly pro-poor language while BJP and TDP skew toward market and growth vocabulary.

BJP’s Language Has Shifted Over Time

The Hindutva score — BJP’s average position on the nationalist dimension — increases monotonically from the 16th to the 18th Lok Sabha.

The shift is not dramatic, but it is consistent. The language BJP MPs choose when questioning government ministries has moved measurably toward religious and civilisational vocabulary over a decade in government.

Parties Question Different Things

LDA topic models identify 15 recurring themes. The heatmap below shows how strongly each party emphasises each topic.

BJP concentrates on infrastructure and defence; INC on governance and accountability; Left parties on labour and wages. Regional parties show sharp, idiosyncratic peaks — which makes sense, since their mandate is narrow and local.

Document Map: UMAP

Each of the 205 party-session documents projected into 2D from the full 3,258-term TF-IDF space.

BJP and INC form the two largest, most central clusters. Left parties cluster tightly. Regional parties scatter to the periphery, consistent with their state-specific focus.

Reproducing the Analysis

Requirements: R 4.3+. Everything else is installed automatically.

source("code/_master.R")

Downloads data, installs packages, and runs the full pipeline A1 through A8. Takes roughly 15 minutes on a modern laptop.
